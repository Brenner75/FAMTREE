Brenner Juliane was born in the zodiac sign of Leo on a Friday, the 22.08.1884 in Gaisruck as daughter of
  • Fehringer Franz, born in 1844, a_sheperd from Gettsdorf
  • and Haslinger Theresia, born in 1847 of Niederrussbach
Gaisruck is a little village in the northern part of Austria.

She had 7 bretheren: Franz, Karl, Barbara, born in 1870, Therese, Marie, Katharina and Anna. She married at the age of 25 years in the year 1909 in Hausleiten Brenner Carl Richard -, 2 years and 2 months older -, a_milk trader, born in 1882 from Stockerau. She was a_grocery shop owner and the couple lived in Wien_Capistrangasse. They had 2 children: Karl Franz, born in 1910 and Franz, born in 1913. In the year 1920 she mourned the death of her husband Carl.

Not much is known about her early life, but after the much too early death of her husband (she had already become a widow at the age of 36), who had died just 3 weeks after her father, she ran a dairy shop in Capistrangasse in Wien Mariahilf to raise her two sons Karl and Franz. She was a very proud woman and they say that she only greeted her customers when they entered her shop, but never when she met them on the street. She was a stately appearance with interesting green-gray eyes and loved to wear a fox fur in winter, which nestled around her neck and bit her own tail.

Her sister-in-law Karoline, who also lived with her, helped her run the dairy business. Her granddaughter also lived with her, while she lived in the nearby humanistic Gymnasium in Rahlgasse and thus saved the long way to school from German-Wagram. The apartment (in which an original rocking chair of the Viennese company Thonet stood -which is still in use today in the family by her grandson) was located on the ground floor with a beautiful view of a courtyard where from time to time you can hear the vocal exercises of the Burgschauspielers Josef Meinrad, who also lived there.

She regularly burned her little moustache with a candle before she met a Mr. Brillinger, who was probably a kind of partner of hers in the later years, but hardly ever appeared and was very restrained.

In her old age she was severely diabetic and got an open wound on her leg that did not want to heal and ultimately led to her death.

She died at the age of 77 years on a Wednesday, the 04.10.1961 in Wien 41 years later than her husband of diabetes. Mourning were amongst others her son Karl, her granddaughter Hertha Zobl, her brother in law Franz, her sister in law Karoline, her daughter in law Alice, her nephews Franz Rauscher and Karl.

She lived in the epoch of Historicism and as a young girl under the reign of the Emperor Franz-Joseph I of Habsburg-Lothringen. Some of her contemporaries of a similar age are Mata Hari and Agatha Christie. During her life there were many wars, amongst them the Great war, the Second World war and the Korean war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during her time are the motorbike, the Aspirin pills,, the spark plug and the thermops flask.
