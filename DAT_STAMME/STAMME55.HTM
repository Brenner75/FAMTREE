She had 5 bretheren: Karl, born in 1871, Josef, born in 1873, Johanna, born in 1875, Hans, born in 1877 and Michael, born in 1880. The father died when little Emilia was 11 years old. She married at the age of 22 years in the year 1891 in Baden_St._Helena Mock Albrecht Johann -, 2 years and 6 months older -, a_Director of a post office, born in 1867 from Wien. She was a_housewife and the couple lived in Deutsch_Wagram. About 1 years later had her daughter Alice, born in 1892. In the year 1944 she mourned the death of her husband Albrecht.

Emilie was the oldest child and had to raise her four lively brothers after the early death of her father, while her sister had lung disease and had also died early. The coffin was transported on a carriage and Emilie was allowed to sit on the carriage box, which she liked very much.

Otherwise her youth was not nice - she had to wash laundry in the icy stream, and often defend her little brothers on the long way to school sometimes with the leather strap of their school bags. She was robbed while shopping for the inn ("go little one, I'll hold your sausage basket while you get me cigarettes") and had to go to the ball as the only girl with a gray dress, where everyone else wore white. Once she wanted to make her mother happy and pick her up from the train station in Baden - but to do so she had to put all her brothers in one pram and received a severe beating from her mother.

She also had to work as a waitress in the inn of the mother in Baden, where the students often got credit from her thanks to her kindness. There she met her admirer Karl Dorrek, but she did not give in to him (but he later adopted her granddaughter Hertha) but favoured her future husband Albrecht.

She was a kind-hearted soul and cared for everyone and everyone. She even visited her brother Pepi who had taken over the inn from the mother, every week in the mountain retreat on the Schoepfel mountain (which he had taken over) and brought him beef filet and cream for his guests. When her mother died, she sent a telegram to all the brothers with the wording: "mother dead, me too".

As a young woman, she was treated like a servant by her domineering step-mother-in-law, but never complained about it and begged even her resolute daughter, who often defended her: "Don't say anything about it!".

After the death of her beloved Albrecht she lived in the same household in Deutsch-Wagram and was mainly responsible for the daily "boiling" of the milk. She also took care of the enlightenment of her granddaughter, but fell asleep in time as a precaution when the topics became too sensitive.

While she was caring for her terminally ill son-in-law, she had a dizzy spell and fell down the cellar stairs, where she wasonly found after a few hours. She had broken her femoral neck, which was practically a death sentence at the time, as patients usually couldn't get back on their feet. However, her daughter forced her to go for a long walks with the pram of her newborn grandchild in the garden until she had recovered from her accident.

She then lived for a few more years and had a wonderful relationship with her little lifesaver.

She died at the age of 87 years on a Sunday, the 04.08.1957 in Deutsch_Wagram 13 years later than her husband of pneumonia. Mourning were amongst others her daughter Alice Koerber, her granddaughters Alice Brenner and Hertha Seil, her great granddaughter Hertha Zobl, her brother Hans.

She lived in the epoch of Historicism and as a young girl under the reign of the Emperor Franz-Joseph I of Habsburg-Lothringen. Some of her contemporaries of a similar age are Marie Curie, Rosa Luxemburg and Mata Hari. During her life there were the Great war, the Second World war and the Korean war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during her time are Blue Jeans, the spark plug, the thermops flask and the 'Ludo' game.
