Koerber Emil was born in the zodiac sign of Leo on a Tuesday, the 21.08.1888 in Spannberg as son of
  • Koerber Emil, born in 1864, a_school master from Jaegerndorf
  • and Schmarda Wilhelmine, born in 1863, a_craft teacher of Spannberg
Spannberg is a little village amongst vinyards in the northern part of Austria (Niederoesterreich).

He had 3 bretheren: Ernst, born in 1890, Emma, born in 1893 and Elisabeth, born in 1901. He married at the age of 25 years in the year 1913 in Wien Mock Alice Emilia Anna -, 4 years younger -, a_housewife, born in 1892 from Baden. He was a_teacher and the couple lived in Deutsch_Wagram. They had 2 children: Alice Anna Anna, born in 1916 and Hertha Wilhelmine, born in 1914.

Little is known about his youth, he came from a family of teachers - his father was headmaster in Parbasdorf and his mother was a handicraft teacher.

1909 he was a substitute teacher and had to help out in 35 different schools, due to the constant travels he was more at home in inns and wine cellars than in his unheated quarters. In Siebenbrunn he earned more by playing the organ in the church than by his teacher's salary. After church, he went to the inn, ate a goulash and drank - according to his own statement - his 30 pints of beer. On a journey home from a party trip (where his future father-in-law Albrecht Mock was also present), the horse-drawn carriage overturned broke his tibia and fibula. A visit to his future wife in the hospital left him so speechless that Alice almost never wanted to see him again. However, he became engaged at Christmas 1912 and married her shortly afterwards. At that time, he was already a teacher in Neusiedl on the Czech border. At first he didn't care much about his wife, who was very sick and the marriage was a disaster at the beginning.

It was only after the birth of his second daughter that he remembered his duties as a family man. When his wife bowled out both arms and labored on them for 12 years, he even learned to to set it up again - once even completely unnoticed during a concert visit in Munich. In 1916 he was in the military, but thanks to his leg injury he managed to squat mostly in the living room. In Neusiedl there was nothing left to eat after the war, so he moved with his family to the Parents to German-Wagram.

1919 he then received a permanent position as a teacher in Aderklaa. He was involved as an organist, community secretary, insurance agent and probably also a bit with the emerging Nazi Party.

In 1942 the school in Aderklaa was closed due to a lack of children and he was forced to move with his family back to his parents in Wagram. In 1945 he was drafted into the Volkssturm. After the war, there was great famine - he leased a field in Strasshof, where he grew vegetables, but they were stolen before the harvest. He imported geese from Poland, which his mother-in-law bought, tried his hand at chicken-rabbit and beekeeping -but not always successfully! Since he was considered Nazi sypathiser he did not receive a pension.

Times were very hard. At the end of the 40s, he increasingly lost weight, his schoolmates no longer recognized him at a graduation ceremony, but refused to consult a doctor, although his wife vehemently urged him to do so. He said to her: "You're always sick, I still like the food". In the end, however, nothing helped and he was hospitalized, diagnosed with prostate cancer and operated on immediately. Since he couldn't resist tinkering with his catheter, he got a sepsis and received 8 blood transfusions - but it continued to go downhill with him.

When he was back home for a short time, he began to hallucinate, he saw a maharaja and believed that valuable oil stocks were hidden under the bed. His second daughter who was pregnant at the time was afraid of him. His mother-in-law, who cared for him, fell daown the stairs tot he cellar and broke her femoral neck and so they both ended up in the Lainz hospital, where he died a short time later. He had not lived to see his second grandchild.

He died at the age of 60 years on a Monday, the 31.01.1949 in Wien 22 years earlier than his wife of kidney failure. Mourning were amongst others his wife Alice, his daughters Alice Brenner and Hertha Seil, his son in law Karl Brenner.

He lived in the epoch of Historicism and as a young fellow under the reign of the Emperor Franz-Joseph I of Habsburg-Lothringen. Some of his contemporaries of a similar age are Albert Einstein, Franz Marc and Picasso. During his life there were the Great war and the Second World war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during his time are the Diesel engine, the television, trains on magnetic rails and the jet engine.
