Scholz Maria was born in 1825 in Oesterreich. Oesterreich (Austria) is a state in the heart of Europe with 8.8 Mio inhabitants.

She was married to Scholz Karl -, 26 years older -, a_glove maker, born in 1820 from Wien. She was a_washing woman and the couple lived in Wien. They had 5 children: Elisabeth, born in 1845, Theresia, born in 1849, Karl, born in 1846, Anna, born in 1847 and Wilhelm, born in 1850.

She was married to a glove maker who had a shop in Kaerntnerstrasse in Wien (or in Rotenturmstrasse?). He died early and she was alone with 5 children. To feed the family, she washed and ironed for "fine gentlemen". Her eldest daughter marries the dashing "Schaani von der Wieden" at the age of 20, the elder son became a master stonemason and moved to Berlin, where his trace is lost, the two girls Theresia and Anna went to the ballet school - whereby Theresia even became prima ballerina at the Vienna State Opera and later was very wealthy and the youngest son became city architect with a beautiful villa in Salzburg right on the river. Fortunately, the children thanked her for these self-sacrificing efforts - in her old age she was taken in by Wilhelm in Salzburg in his house - while Therese offered her a lifelong pension of 1200 guilders. She gave most of it away to her great-granddaughter, who liked to visit her in Salzburg. There she died when the Salzach was flooded, probably in 1909.

The exact date of death is not known, but she died propably at the age of 84 years in the year 1909.

She lived in the epoch of Classicism and as a young girl under the reign of the Emperor Ferdinand I of Habsburg-Lothringen. During her life there were many wars, amongst them the Sardinian-Austrian war, the Hungarian Revolution and the Sardinian war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during her time are the raw material for the paper production, the tram, the motorbike and the car.
