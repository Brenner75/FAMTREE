  • Mock Johann Baptist, born in 1843, a_line drawer from Wien
  • and Scholz Elisabeth, born in 1845 of Wien Alservorstadt
Wien (Vienna) is the capital of Austria and you may look at some photos here.

He had 2 bretheren: (R2146), born in 1868 and (R2145), born in 1866. The mother died when little Albrecht was 3 years old. He married at the age of 24 years in the year 1891 in Baden_St._Helena Binder Emilia -, 2 years and 6 months younger -, a_housewife, born in 1869 from Wien. He was a_Director of a post office and the couple lived in Deutsch-Wagram. About 1 years later had her daughter Alice, born in 1892.

Since his mother had died at an early age, he had a difficult childhood under the care of his evil stepmother. But then he was mainly looked after by his Aunt Theresia Scholz, who was a State Opera dancer. She wanted to finance his career as an officer, but this was thwarted by his stepmother because she did not like the "noble relatives".

During his military service in Baden, he met his future wife Emilie in the inn and later married her against strong competition from his student friends. Since he had a good figure, he was once asked by his superior colonel if he wore a bodice. He was so excited about this that he pulled out his saber and almost came before a court-martial. Thankfully, however, this episode had no further consequences.

As a child he had smallpox and lay blind in bed for 7 days and therefore bore many scars, but his wife didn't mind that ("nice patching is also beautiful").

Since his parents wanted to thwart the marriage with Emilie, he broke with them - he was supposed to marry a daughter of the landlord of Moedlingen. They did not appear at his wedding. It was only at the baptism of his daughter that there was a kind of reconciliation. However, his wife was treted by her step-in-law but like a servant.

After the death of his aunt, he inherited 5000 guilders and thus added a room and a veranda to the 040 house in Deutscg-Wagram, which his parents had acquired shortly before. He inherited again from his grandmother and expanded the house in 1904 by three more rooms to its present state. He was one of the first to use electric light and had a water pipe introduced, and bright chandeliers burned in all the rooms.

With his beautiful bass voice he was a member of the Gesangsverein in Deutsch-Wagram and sang with his childhood friend Karl Dorrek and the gentlemen Schwetter and Strasser in the Vernay Quartet. They gave many guest performances and traveled to the Turkey. On the island of Brioni, he once helped a lady to find a piece of jewellery that had fallen into the water on the quay. He dived so long that one thought he would never come up again. But he found the jewellery and was thus the hero of the day.

In raising his only daughter he was rigorous and set up the following timetable for her: In the morning you have to operate the duster and the wooden spoon, after the meal it is time to wash dishes and then 2 hours are yours, you can read, write letters, visit friends or whatever makes you happy, then you learn to mend, knit, darn, do you need it yourself, is it good for you, if you have servants, you must know better than they do, or you will be sold.

His grandchildren Alice and Hertha grew up with him and his wife and were brought up in the same way - especially to be punctual. Even if they were only late by one minute, he was already at the door with the pocket watch. One day they wanted to run away because of the strict upbringing and the whole family cried, he only said: "Let the stupid children go!".

He was a member of the municipal council, the association for the construction of the Deutsch-Wagram War Memorial and significantly involved in the construction of the lido.

Two days before his death, his daughter was still with him and fed him pineapple strawberries. He stroked her hair and said, "You were a good child and you've always given me nothing but joy. Due to a persistent air raid alarm, she was unable to visit him afterwards.

He died at the age of 77 years on a Tuesday, the 27.06.1944 in DeutschWagram 13 years earlier than his wife of lung cancer. Mourning were amongst others his wife Emilia, his daughter Alice Koerber, his granddaughters Alice Brenner and Hertha Seil, his brother in law Hans Binder, his son in law Emil Koerber.

He lived in the epoch of Historicism and as a young fellow under the reign of the Emperor Franz-Joseph I of Habsburg-Lothringen. Some of his contemporaries of a similar age are Richard Strauss, Wassily Kandinsky and Mahatma Gandhi. During his life there were the Great war and the Second World war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during his time are Blue Jeans, the tape recorder, the tea bag and the television.
