Mock Anna was born in the zodiac sign of Leo on a Sunday, the 06.08.1809 in Bad_Hall as daughter of
  • Windhager Josef, born in 1760, a_tailor from Pfarrkirchen
  • and Prenner (Brenner) Rosalia (Rosina), born in 1765 of Pfarrkirchen
Bad_Hall is located in Upper Austria.

She married at the age of 28 years in the year 1837 in Wien Mock Thomas -, 26 years older -, a_watch maker, born in 1782 from Wien III. The couple lived in Deutsch-Wagram. They had 2 children: Johann Baptist, born in 1843 and Josefa Alexandrina, born in 1842. In the year 1846 she mourned the death of her husband Thomas.

She was wed in second marriage to Mr. Hutzler, a_painter and decorator.

She and her husband were citizens from Vienna, certainly well situated, which can be concluded from the jewellery on the couple's oil pictures. The pictures were painted by the brother of the first wife of her husband.

After the birth of two children and after the death of her much older husband, she married a second time and gave this husband 3 more children. But he must not have been a solid man, because it seems as if she spent her last days as a widow with her son in German-Wagram, in financial worries. From two jars with Theresientaler, one glass is already missing the thaler, the second already shows traces of etching.

At the age of 91, she fell asleep peacefully. At 2 o'clock in the morning she asked for another slice of vanilla, ate half of it and lay dead in bed in the morning. At their funeral, where only very few people were present, there was such a black ice that the coffin bearers fell over. She is buried in German-Wagram in the old cemetery in Sahulka park opposite the Napoleon Memorial Chapel. After the establishment of the new cemetery, most of the bones were exhumed from there and brought to the new cemetery - but not those of Anna Windhager: her son could not or did not want to pay the expenses for it.

She died at the age of 91 years on a Friday, the 04.01.1901 in Deutsch-Wagram 54 years later than her husband. Mourning were amongst others her son Johann, her grandchild Albrecht.

She lived in the epoch of Classicism and as a young girl under the reign of the Emperor Franz II of Habsburg-Lothringen. During her life there were many wars, amongst them the Bitish-American war, Napoleon's war in Russia and Napoleoen's Summer wars. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during her time are the mouth organ, the record player, the Diesel engine and the glider (aeroplane).
