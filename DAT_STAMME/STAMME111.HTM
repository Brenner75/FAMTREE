Binder Anna was born in the zodiac sign of Pisces on a Saturday, the 22.02.1845 in Budapest as daughter of
  • Gindra Anton, born in 1810 from Ungarn
  • and Bloszovitz Magdalena, born in 1820 of Budapest
Budapest is the Hungarian capital.

She had 2 bretheren: Hermine, born in 1849 and Fanny. She married at the age of 25 years on a Saturday, the 16.10.1870 in Baden_St._Stefan Binder Josef Johann -, 4 years older -, a_waiter, born in 1840 from Wien Fuenfhaus. She was a_pub landlady and the couple lived in Baden. They had 6 children: Emilia, born in 1869, Karl, born in 1871, Josef, born in 1873, Johanna, born in 1875, Hans, born in 1877 and Michael, born in 1880. In the year 1880 she mourned the death of her husband Josef. In the year 1913 she stood by the grave of her child Johanna.

She and her sister Mina were orphans and had to register immediately as a maids. They were so badly fed by a Mayer family in Vienna that they went away and left a note: "The peas and lentils drove us away, otherwise we would have stayed longer at Mayer's."

Unlike her sister who went to Holy Mass every day, she had the attitude: "I am not doing anything wrong and God is everywhere!"

She met her husband - a pay waiter - in Vienna and immediately had an illegitimate child from him. Only a year later they married in St. Stefan in Baden near Vienna and had little Emilie registered in the marriage. After that, the family moved to Baden and leased the community inn in Leesdorf. When she was 36, her husband died and she was left alone with 6 children. She had her eldest Daughter from school at the age of 11 so that she could help her raise the lively brothers. The boys were probably real bullies and she often had to pay for damage to smashed windows - often with a calming crumb or quarter in the inn to the Sandwirt, which she ran at that time. At that time there was no curfew and she often had to stay with a drunk guest until the early morning hours.

For the sake of the children, she remained unmarried, although she had received many requests from guests. Unfortunately, she was not thanked for this, because when she handed over the business to her eldest son Pepi, she was thrown out oft he house by her daughter-in-law and had to move from one child to another in her old age, wherever she was needed. This was with her daughter Johanna the case, who lived with her husband and 5 children in Martonvasar in Hungary on the farm of Anton Drechsler, where her husband was stable master for over 80 horses and 10 carriages.

Often she had enough of this life and traveled - completely without luggage and spontaneously - to her grandchild in German-Wagram.

She died at the age of 71 years on a Friday, the 30.06.1916 in Baden about 36 years later than her husband. Mourning were amongst others her son Hans, her daughter Emilia Mock, her granddaughter Alice Koerber, her son in law Albrecht Mock.

She lived in the epoch of Historicism and as a young girl under the reign of the Emperor Franz-Joseph I of Habsburg-Lothringen. During her life there were many wars, amongst them the Sardinian-Austrian war, the Hungarian Revolution and the Sardinian war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during her time are the light bulb, the dynamo, Blue Jeans and the bakteriology.
