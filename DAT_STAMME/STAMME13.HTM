Aderklaa is a little village in the western part (Marchfeld) of Austria.

She had a sister Hertha Wilhelmine, 1 years and 10 months older, born in 1914. She married at the age of 20 years in the year 1936 in Aderklaa Brenner Karl Franz -, 6 years older -, a_civil servant, born in 1910 from Gaisruck. She was a_housewife and the couple lived in Deutsch_Wagram. They had 2 children: Karl Georg Emil, born in 1949 and Hertha, born in 1938. In the year 1993 she mourned the death of her husband Karl.

Together with her older sister she was raised by her grandparents in German-Wagram, while her parents lived in Aderklaa, for the following 2 reasons. If the large house in German-Wagram had only been inhabited by 2 people, then people would certainly have been forcibly quartered there, as there were many homeless families. Secondly, she would have had to go to school in Aderklaa with her own father and they wanted to avoid the need to explain that the A's in the report card were a gift from the father. The grandparents had strict discipline, especially punctuality. Once the "stupid children" even wanted to run away.

As a young girl she often had to buy from a butcher who had a large St. Bernard dog on a chain, of which she was very afraid. And indeed, one day the dog broke free, pursued her, and would have torn her to pieces if the master of the house had not intervened. The resulting fear of dogs still lives on today some of their descendants.

In contrast to her sister, who was allowed to go to the teacher training academy, in her time the need was so great and the money so scarce that she worked with her grandfather at the post office had to be accommodated.

When her sister found a dashing piano player in the fraternity, however, she won the race and married the "bald man" shortly afterwards. The family then moved with the daughter born in 1938 to Hainfeld in a beautiful Villa. Then the Second World War broke out and she had to go on with the child adventurous way to flee from the imminent invasion of the Russians towards the end of the war to Lower Bavaria, where her sister was married.

Her husband, who was in Russian captivity, returned in 1946 and the family was happily reunited in German-Wagram in the house of her parents and grandparents, where they lived until 1954.

With the unexpected latecomer born in 1949, she and the family then moved to Wien, where she lived briefly in a rented apartment of her sister. In your first own flat in Wien Favoriten she devoted herself entirely to the management of an upper middle class household with everything that went with it.

After her two children left home (first her daughter who went to America, and later her son who emigrated to England), she and her husband were always drawn to the house more to German-Wagram, where the house was empty after the death of her mother. There she was very involved in a religious association - the Cursillo. Some of her 010 thoughts from that time are still preserved in writing.

After the death of her husband she became very enterprising and undertook many long-distance trips with her daughter to China, India, Russia, etc.

When her mental strength waned, she moved into a retirement home near Schoenbrunn, where she spent several years in top physical shape until her peaceful death.

She died at the age of 96 years on a Monday, the 01.10.2012 in Wien 19 years later than her husband of old age. Mourning were amongst others her son Karl, her daughter Hertha Zobl, her grandchildren Michael, Thomas Zobl and XXX XXX, her granddaughter Martina Askell, her great grandchildren Samuel Askell and Charlie Askell, her son in law Engelbert Zobl.

She lived in the epoch of modern times and as a young girl under the reign of the Emperor Karl I of Habsburg-Lothringen. Some of her contemporaries of a similar age are Astrid Lindgren and Mutter Theresa. During her life there were many wars, amongst them the Second World war, the Korean war and the Vietnam war.
