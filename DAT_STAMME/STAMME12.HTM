Brenner Karl was born in the zodiac sign of Aquarius on a Sunday, the 06.02.1910 in Gaisruck as son of
  • Brenner Carl Richard, born in 1882, a_milk trader from Stockerau
  • and Fehringer Juliane, born in 1884, a_grocery shop owner of Gaisruck
Gaisruck is a little village in the northern part of Austria.

He had a brother Franz, 2 years and 10 months younger, born in 1913. The father died when little Karl was 10 years old. He married at the age of 26 years in the year 1936 in Aderklaa Koerber Alice Anna Anna -, 6 years younger -, a_housewife, born in 1916 from Aderklaa. He was a_civil servant and the couple lived in Wien. They had 2 children: Karl Georg Emil, born in 1949 and Hertha, born in 1938.

He attended elementary school first in Pettendorf, then in stockerau, then the Realgymnasium there until 1929. He studied geography and history at the university in Wien from 1929-1935. his notes about it are still almost completely preserved.

He was a member of the student fraternity Nordgau and played the piano in the evenings with the "Couleurdamen", where he was discovered by Hertha - one of the Koerber Maedchen. In the end, however, he married her younger sister Alice after he - according to Albrecht Mock - had "looked at the cow in the barn". At that time he was already employed in the welfare office in Wolkersdorf for 120 shillings a month.

In 1937 he was pragmatized in Hainfeld as a district office assistant and the couple moved into a Villa there. Shortly after the birth of his daughter who was born on 29.6.1939 in st. Poelten he was conscripted to the Wehrmacht. He was an accountant for supplies in the Polish campaign of 1939, later also in Holland, Belgium, France, Romania, Bulgaria, Serbia and Greece.

1941 he was captured and sent to Russia in a cattle car. (He stood at the train station for 3 days in German-Wagram and smuggled out a note "Don't know where my family is.") He was jailed in the Ukraine for 2 years. He was released because of an injury to the shin caused by a cow (he had loved cows ever since).

After the war and the unexpected birth of his son in 1949, he was an auditor in the Lower Austrian provincial government, later a government councillor and head of a Department. He and his family first lived in German Wagram, then lived in a rented flat belongin to his sister-in-law in Wien 1, then in their first own flat in Vienna Favoriten, later they bought an apartment in the second district of Vienna in Schiffamtsgasse near his city office. After his retirement, he lived again in German-Wagram, where he continued to play the piano and lovingly tended the garden until his death.

He died at the age of 83 years on a Saturday, the 06.02.1993 in Wien 19 years earlier than his wife of a stroke. Mourning were amongst others his wife Alice, his son Karl, his daughter Hertha Zobl, his grandchildren Michael, Thomas Zobl and XXX XXX, his granddaughter Martina Askell, his sister in law Hertha Seil, his son in law Engelbert Zobl, his daughter in law Franziska, his nephew Kurt, his cousin Franz Rauscher as well as further members of the family.

He lived in the epoch of modern times and as a young fellow under the reign of the Emperor Karl I of Habsburg-Lothringen. Some of his contemporaries of a similar age are Louis Armstrong, Walt Disney and Salvadoire Dali. During his life there were many wars, amongst them the Great war, the Second World war and the Korean war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during his time are the 'Goldbaerchen' sweet, the famouse 'curry wurst' in Berlin, the football boots and the wall plug.
