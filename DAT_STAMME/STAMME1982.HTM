The exact date of birth is not known, but M llner Karoline was propably born 1850 in Wien. Wien (Vienna) is the capital of Austria and you may look at some photos here.

She had a sister (R1946). She was a_fashion maker and lived in Deutsch-Wagram.

She was wed in second marriage to Mock Johann Baptist, a_line drawer.

She was a milliner and had a paper shop at Wiedner Hauptstrasse 82. She fell in love with the "dashing Schani von der Wieden" while he was still married. His wife was already very ill and one day she was taken out of the ballroom by the mother-in-law of the dashing Schani, got a slap in the face and heard: "Wait - until she dies!" After their marriage, however, the fun life for the Schani continued, he left her and he fled to Romania.

She had no time for the "noble relatives", and thwarted hence the ambitions of Therese Scholz, who would have financed an officer's career for her stepson. With the help of the paper business, she was just able to keep herself and her stepson afloat. Only when her husband was brought back by his brother-in-law and accommodated with the imperial Northern Railway, did she rest in her life and she then led a reasonably good marriage, went to church on Sundays with her husband and helped to clean the jewelry every Saturday. She always had a maid, until the time when her children moved in with her.

She was very domineering and at that time she was already living in Deutsch-Wagram together with her stepson and his wife, who she treated her like a servant, since she had always been against this marriage. To the rebukes of her resolute granddaughter she only always replied: "Be quiet, you cheeky beak or you'll get a Tetschen (slap in th face)!".

After the death of her husband she had a Jewish friend who managed to wrest almost the entire inheritance promised to the children from her. Among them was a double-mantled watch from the Paris World's Fair with an agate set in gold and a brilliant-cut diamond as well as a beautiful table with the finest inlays on the biblical story. Only the two ancestral images, the grandfather clock and the mortgage for the house remained for her heirs.

She died at the age of 73 years on a Tuesday, the 22.05.1923.

She lived in the epoch of Historicism and as a young girl under the reign of the Emperor Franz-Joseph I of Habsburg-Lothringen. During her life there were many wars, amongst them the Sardinian war, the Italien Independence war and teh German-Danish war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during her time are the light bulb, the motorbike, the car and the record player.
