While her workers carried the large 0piano bodies, she was allowed to follow with the piano feet.

She was a cub leader in the scout group 35, where she also met her future husband in 1966. They married 3 years later and she gave birth to her daughter in 1970, almost in a truck.

Shortly after the birth of her son she followed her husband with the whole family to England, where they settled in Poole, a town on the south coast. After a few years as a housewife and "nanny", she helped her husband start a joint IT company, where she was the finance director. She continued to be involved in the Boy Scouts and was Akela in the cub pack of St. Peter's Church. She organized fantastic parties for friends and neighbors with Austrian cuisine in her 050 house in Wedgwood Drive.

In the late 80s, the business failed in the general recession and she had the thankless task of selling all her possessions and handing over the keys to her 050 house at the bank. At that time, her husband had already emigrated to Germany as an "economic refugee", where he got a job in Muenchen. She and her son followed a few months later while her daughter married the "neighbor boy" Joe.

She then lived in the heart Muenchens for a few years before she fell seriously ill with lung cancer. She endured her illness with incredible patience and died a few days after her 50th birthday in 1997 with her husband at her side. She is buried in Wien in the grave of her parents at the Inzersdorf cemetery.
