From the military academy he came in 1899 as a lieutenant in the 4th Tyrolean Kaiserjaeger Regiment. 
He embarked on a career in the General Staff and was finally promoted to Colonel in the General Staff on 1 November 1918.
In 1918/1919 he was local commander in Salzburg.
In August 1920, Richard Schilhawsky took over the command of the officers' course in Vienna, and from 1923 to 1925 he headed 
(promoted to Major General in 1924) was Department 4 of the Federal Ministry of the Army, and in 1925 was given command 
of the 4th Brigade Upper Austria, became General and Army Inspector on 1 March 1926 and retired on 23 December 1928.
